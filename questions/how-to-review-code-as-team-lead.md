# How do I review code in a way that raises the bar without making people defensive?

## Why this is open

I noticed this week that one developer's PR quality jumped sharply when he knew that the architect would review it. On the surface this is good. But it might mean reviews now feel like exams, which is the wrong dynamic — exams produce performance, not learning.

I do not yet have a model for separating "high standards" from "high stakes."

## What I currently believe

- Comments framed as questions ("what happens if X is null?") work better than comments framed as corrections.
- Praising specific decisions, not "nice work," gives the reviewee a signal of what to repeat.
- Reviewing my *own* PRs out loud in front of the team might lower the stakes of being reviewed.

## What I want to learn

- Are there written sources on this? Search: Google's engineering practices doc, "The Art of Readable Code," any post-mortems from senior engineers on review culture.
- How do the seniors I respect review my code? Re-read recent comments on my PRs and look for the pattern.

## When I'll close this

When I have written a `notes/` entry on my own review philosophy that I would defend to a skeptical engineer.
