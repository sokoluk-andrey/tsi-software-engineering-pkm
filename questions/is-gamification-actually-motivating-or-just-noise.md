# Is gamification in productivity tools actually motivating, or is it just noise?

## Why this is open

TaskMaster's pitch leans on gamification — achievements, rewards, progress feedback. I picked the feature because it sounds appealing and because I have seen apps like Habitica do it. But I have not actually checked whether the research supports it, or whether it just *feels* motivating during onboarding and then becomes wallpaper.

If gamification is wallpaper after week two, then putting it at the center of the product is a strategic mistake, not just an aesthetic one.

## What I want to find out

- Is there academic research on long-term retention effects of gamification in productivity apps (not games)?
- What did Habitica, Todoist Karma, and Forest do — and what does their churn look like?
- Is there a version of "gamification" that survives the novelty effect? (Streaks? Loss-aversion mechanics?)

## When I'll close this

When I have either a defended design choice for TaskMaster's reward system or a defended decision to drop it. Either is acceptable; "I included it because it sounded good" is not.
